# Offers & Contracts — Saudi Labor Market

## HRDF (Human Resources Development Fund) Support
- Also known as Hadaf
- Covers up to 75% of training costs for eligible Saudi candidates
- Provides salary subsidies for first 2 years of employment
- Tamheer program: 3000 SAR/mo stipend for on-the-job training
- Hafiz program: 2000 SAR/mo allowance during job search (up to 12 months)
- Funding application process: Employer submits → HRDF reviews → Approved/Rejected (5-10 business days)

## Typical Offer Components
- Base salary (SAR/month)
- Housing allowance (common in Saudi offers, 15-25% of base)
- Transportation allowance
- Annual bonus (1-3 months)
- Medical insurance (mandatory — employer-provided)
- End of service benefits (as per Saudi Labor Law)
- Training budget (increasingly common in tech sector)
- Relocation support (for candidates from other cities)

## Salary Ranges by Sector (SAR/month)
- Technology Entry: 8K-12K | Mid: 15K-22K | Senior: 22K-35K
- Healthcare Entry: 7K-10K | Mid: 12K-18K | Senior: 18K-30K
- Finance Entry: 10K-15K | Mid: 18K-25K | Senior: 25K-40K
- Energy Entry: 10K-15K | Mid: 18K-28K | Senior: 28K-45K
- Tourism Entry: 6K-9K | Mid: 10K-16K | Senior: 16K-25K

## Contract Types
- Indefinite (open-ended) — most common for Saudi nationals
- Fixed-term (1-2 years) — common for project-based roles
- Probation period: typically 90 days (extendable to 180)
- Notice period: 30-60 days depending on contract

## Saudization (Nitaqat) Context
- Employers must maintain minimum Saudi employee ratios
- Categories: Platinum, Green (High/Mid/Low), Yellow, Red
- Candidates benefit from Saudization — employers actively seek Saudi talent
- HRDF incentivizes hiring Saudis through wage subsidies
